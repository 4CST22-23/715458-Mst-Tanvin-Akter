Six Season

Introducing of six season:

Although the number of seasons in most countries of the world is four, Bangladesh is a country of six seasons. Here a new season emerges every two months. The seasons are – Summer, Monsoon, Autumn, Winter, Winter and Spring. They rotate in cycles. And with the advent of every season, the nature and beauty of Bangladesh becomes diverse.

Summer

Summer comes at the beginning of the seasonal cycle. Baisakh-Jyashta is two months of summer. The arrival of summer to burn fields and fields with fire torches. Then the sky and air became dusty. Nature's green-smooth form is lost. Canals, rivers and canals dry up. In the unbearable heat, all animals yearn for a little cool water and shade.In the meantime, sometimes Kalbaisakhi's violent rampage starts suddenly. Everything breaks down. But summer does not only burn, but gives to unsparing hands nectar fruits like mango, jam, jackfruit, jamrul, litchi, watermelon and coconut.

Monsoon season:

Monsoon comes in Mahasamaroh after summer. Ashad-Shravan is two months of rainy season. Varsha appeared in the guise of a victorious warrior. Nature trembled at the thunderous roar of the clouds. It started raining in torrents. The fields are filled with water. A pleasant liveliness appears in nature.Peace returns to public life. Farmers plant paddy-jute seeds in the land. Kadam, keya, jasmine bloom on trees. Fruits like pineapple, guava, amra, kamaranga, latkon etc. are available in monsoon.

Autumn:

Autumn comes with the fragrance of Shiuli flowers in the air. Bhadra-Ashwin is two months of autumn. At this time, white clouds float like cotton in the blue sky. A fair of white flowers sits on the banks of the river. Rows of white bucks fly in garlands in the afternoon. Paddy fields swayed by green waves.The fair of countless stars shines in the night sky. The water in the bill sparkles with Shapla's smile. So the poet sang— 

                    Today is your sweet idol
                        Herinu Sharad Prabhate.
                      O Mother, Bengal, green limbs
                          Amal shines brightly.

Autumn is called the queen of seasons because of this beautiful form of autumn.

Late Autumn:

Late Autumn comes with the joy of Navanna festival from home to home. Kartik-Agrahayana two months Hemantkala. The form of Hemanta in nature is yellow. In summer, the chest of the field is covered with flowers. Ripe rice in the field. Farmers are busy harvesting crops. The farmer's bowl is filled with golden rice, a smile of joy bursts on his face.The festival of Navanna begins. Hemant comes in silence; Again it is hidden behind the winter fog.

Winter:

Winter comes with the northerly wind on the blanket of fog. Paush-Magh is two months of winter. Winter is the season of emptiness. Man and nature become helpless in Konkan winter. However, in a variety of vegetables, fruits and flowers, the gloomy nature is filled. The scent of palm juice floats in the air. Kheer, Pies and Pitha- Puli festival is celebrated in rural Bengal

Springtime:

Spring finally comes to Rajbesh. Phalgun-chaitra is two months of spring. Spring brings the flower festival. The fragrance of mau mau flowers floats in the air. The sweet song of the cuckoo-papaya in the tree. Dakhina wind blows the cold wind.The sound of harmony rings in the human soul. The soulless poet sang in joy— 

             Oh, this spring
  
                So many flowers bloom, so many whistles
  
                   So many birds sing

Conclusion:

In Bangladesh this fun of six seasons is going on endlessly. Different seasons bring out different combinations of forms in nature. His influence fell on the minds of the people of Bengal. Due to the influence of different seasons, the people of Bangladesh are generous and full of love.
